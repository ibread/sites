🍃 Pre-Qingming Longjing

A Precious Green Tea from Hangzhou, China

What is Pre-Qingming Longjing?

Longjing (Dragon Well) is China's most famous green tea, grown around West Lake in Hangzhou. It has over 1,200 years of history and is renowned for its "four perfections": emerald color, aromatic fragrance, sweet taste, and beautiful flat shape.

Pre-Qingming (明前) means the tea leaves were harvested before the Qingming Festival (early April). This is the earliest and most prized harvest of the year.

Why Pre-Qingming Tea is Special

☀️ Four Reasons It's Called "Gold"

  • Tender: After winter dormancy, the buds are incredibly delicate and rich in amino acids, giving a naturally sweet, non-bitter taste
  • Clean: Harvested before pests emerge, requiring virtually no pesticides
  • Rare: The picking window is only 10-15 days per year, with very limited yield
  • Fragrant: Tea plants accumulate aromatic compounds all winter, released in the first spring harvest

"Pre-Qingming tea is a treasure; Yuqian (pre-Grain Rain) tea is a fine product." — Chinese tea proverb

Emperor Qianlong of the Qing Dynasty was so impressed by Longjing that he designated 18 tea bushes at Shifeng Mountain as "Imperial Tea" during his visit to Hangzhou.

About Hangzhou

Hangzhou, the capital of Zhejiang Province, is one of China's most beautiful cities. Marco Polo described it as "the City of Heaven" for its elegance and prosperity.

West Lake (西湖) is the heart of Hangzhou—a UNESCO World Heritage site with classical gardens, ancient temples, and misty hills. The unique microclimate around West Lake (humid, foggy, with acidic soil) creates perfect conditions for growing exceptional tea.

Chinese Tea: A Simple Guide

Chinese tea is categorized by how the leaves are processed, especially the level of oxidation (exposure to air). Here are the six main types:

🟢
Green Tea
Zero oxidation. Fresh, grassy, light. Examples: Longjing, Biluochun
🟡
Yellow Tea
Slight oxidation. Similar to green but with a "sealed yellowing" step. Rare and mellow.
🔵
White Tea
Minimal processing. Dried naturally. Delicate and sweet. Example: Baihaoyinzhen
🟠
Oolong Tea
Partial oxidation (10-70%). Complex, floral, fruity. Examples: Tieguanyin, Da Hong Pao
🔴
Black Tea
Full oxidation (called "Red Tea" in China). Bold, malty. Examples: Keemun, Yunnan Gold
⚫
Dark Tea
Post-fermented. Aged over time. Earthy and smooth. Example: Pu'er

Longjing is a Green Tea — it's pan-fired immediately after picking to stop oxidation, preserving its fresh, vegetal character and bright green color.

How to Brew Longjing

  1. Water temperature: 80-85°C (175-185°F). Never use boiling water—it will scorch the delicate leaves
  2. Ratio: 3g tea per 200ml water (about 1 teaspoon per cup)
  3. First steep: 1-2 minutes. The leaves will dance and unfurl beautifully
  4. Multiple infusions: High-quality Longjing can be steeped 3-4 times, each with a slightly different flavor profile

💡 Tip: Use a clear glass cup to enjoy the visual spectacle of the leaves dancing in the water.
