AI Practice09
An Honest Note

What to hand off — and what to keep.

A simple rule: weigh how much time it eats against how much is at stake.
Stakes → high
🔒 Do it yourself
Quick but critical — not worth the risk of handing off.
⚠️ Do it yourself
Complex and high-stakes — you're often faster doing it than briefing it.
🤷 Either way
Quick & low-stakes — not worth the effort to delegate.
✅ Hand it to AI
Time-consuming, simple, low-stakes. The sweet spot.
summaries · research · first drafts · sync & reports
Time it eats → a lot
🧠 Back to that intern: brilliant but green — gifted & fast, yet stumbles on common sense. Hand off the right work, but always check and sign off.
AI Practice08
Keeping It Real

Where it actually trips up on me.

Real moments — brilliant one minute, oddly clueless the next.
📅
Got the day of the week wrong. Gave me a 10-day forecast but labeled May 18 as Sunday — it was a Monday. Pure common sense, and it whiffed.
🎂
Guessed my kid's age instead of counting. It eyeballed “about 5” from memory rather than doing the math from her birthday — twice. Now it's a hard rule to always calculate.
🧩
Confidently made up “facts.” When building a page it invented real-looking YouTube/photo IDs that didn't exist, and trusted a search engine's wrong publish dates. Anything factual needs a cross-check.
🔘
Forgets small standing instructions. I asked it to always show options as buttons — it still forgets sometimes. Not critical, but a reminder: it needs harnessing, not blind trust.

The Honest Question

“Is it worth babysitting?” Yes — it's an investment.

Harnessing it can be frustrating. I still think now is the best time to start.
😤
The frustration is real. Correcting and steering it sometimes makes me ask: is this even worth my time?
