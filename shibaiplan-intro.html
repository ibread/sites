The Story

Shi Bai Pian (诗百篇), meaning "A Hundred Poems," draws its name from one of China's most beloved literary legends. The Tang Dynasty poet Du Fu immortalized his friend Li Bai—widely regarded as China's greatest poet—in the famous verse describing how Li Bai would compose a hundred magnificent poems after drinking wine.

"With a gallon of wine, Li Bai composes a hundred poems; he sleeps in a tavern in the marketplace of Chang'an. The emperor calls, but he won't board the boat, claiming 'I am an immortal of wine.'" — Du Fu, 8th Century Tang Dynasty

The winery was originally founded as Canaan Wine Estate (迦南酒庄) in 2006 by visionary winemaker Richard Li. In 2019, it was renamed to Shi Bai Pian to better reflect its deep connection to Chinese culture and the belief that great wine, like great poetry, is born from passion, patience, and a touch of divine inspiration.

This cultural heritage is the soul of our wines—each bottle carries the spirit of Li Bai's legendary creativity, inviting you to discover your own poetry in every sip.

The Estate

Vineyards in the foothills of the Yanshan Mountains

📍 Huailai, Hebei Province

Located approximately 150 kilometers northwest of Beijing, Huailai sits at the foot of the Yanshan Mountains at an elevation of 500-800 meters. This unique terroir creates ideal conditions for premium viticulture:

  • Continental Climate: Hot summers, cold winters, and significant day-night temperature variation preserve acidity and develop complex flavors
  • Sandy Loam Soils: Excellent drainage promotes deep root growth and concentrated fruit
  • Mountain Protection: The Yanshan range shields vineyards from harsh northern winds
  • Low Humidity: Reduces disease pressure, enabling sustainable viticulture

Franco-Chinese Heritage

Shi Bai Pian shares a special bond with Domaine Franco-Chinois (中法庄园)—a sister winery that represents the historic collaboration between French and Chinese winemaking traditions. This partnership brings together centuries of Bordeaux expertise with the unique characteristics of Chinese terroir, creating wines that honor both heritages.

Our winemaking philosophy embraces modern techniques while respecting the land. We practice sustainable viticulture, hand-harvest our grapes at optimal ripeness, and age our wines in French oak barrels to develop structure and complexity.
