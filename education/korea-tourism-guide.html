首尔 / 서울 / Seoul

Capital City

Capital city on the Han River, surrounded by mountains including Bukhansan and Namsan. Population ~9.7M. The political, economic, and cultural heart of Korea — a megacity seamlessly blending 600-year-old palaces with cutting-edge modernity.

景福宫 / 경복궁 / Gyeongbokgung 明洞 / 명동 / Myeongdong 北村韩屋村 / 북촌한옥마을 / Bukchon 弘大 / 홍대 / Hongdae 江南 / 강남 / Gangnam 南山塔 / 남산타워 / Namsan Tower 梨泰院 / 이태원 / Itaewon

📖 历史背景 Historical Background

Capital of the Joseon Dynasty since 1394 when Yi Seong-gye (太祖/태조) moved the seat of power here. Home to the Five Grand Palaces (Gyeongbokgung, Changdeokgung, Deoksugung, Changgyeonggung, Gyeonghuigung). Endured Japanese colonial rule (1910–1945), was devastated during the Korean War, then rebuilt into one of Asia's most dynamic megacities through rapid industrialization. The Han River divides the old north (강북) from the modern south (강남).

釜山 / 부산 / Busan

South Korea's 2nd Largest City

Southeastern coastal metropolis and Korea's largest port city. Known for beaches, seafood, film festivals, and a vibrant street culture distinct from Seoul.

海云台 / 해운대 / Haeundae Beach 甘川文化村 / 감천문화마을 / Gamcheon 札嘎其市场 / 자갈치시장 / Jagalchi Market 海东龙宫寺 / 해동용궁사 / Haedong Yonggungsa 广安里 / 광안리 / Gwangalli Beach

📖 历史背景 Historical Background

Served as Korea's temporary capital during the Korean War (1950–53) when Seoul fell. The Busan Perimeter (부산 교두보) was the last line of UN defense in summer 1950. After the war, refugees who settled on the hillsides created what is now the colorful Gamcheon Culture Village. As Korea's largest port since the Joseon era, Busan has long been the gateway for international trade and cultural exchange.

济州岛 / 제주도 / Jeju Island

UNESCO World Heritage

Volcanic island 80km south of mainland Korea with subtropical climate. Formed by Hallasan volcano (1,947m), Korea's highest peak. A UNESCO Triple Crown site — World Heritage, Global Geopark, and Biosphere Reserve.

汉拿山 / 한라산 / Hallasan 城山日出峰 / 성산일출봉 / Seongsan Ilchulbong 牛岛 / 우도 / Udo Island 协才海边 / 협재해변 / Hyeopjae Beach 中文旅游区 / 중문관광단지 / Jungmun Resort 东门市场 / 동문시장 / Dongmun Market

📖 历史背景 Historical Background

Once the independent Tamna Kingdom (탐라국/耽罗国), a self-governing island state until absorbed by Goryeo in 1105. Served as a Mongol horse-breeding base in the 13th century. The tragic April 3rd Incident (제주4·3사건, 1948) — an uprising and brutal suppression — killed tens of thousands and scarred the island for decades. The haenyeo (해녀/海女) free-diving women, with origins spanning centuries, were inscribed on the UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage list in 2016.

江原道 / 강원도 / Gangwon-do

#1 Domestic Destination

Northeastern province with dramatic mountains and the pristine East Sea coastline. Host of the 2018 PyeongChang Winter Olympics. Korea's undisputed #1 domestic travel destination with 21.6% of all accommodation spending.

雪岳山 / 설악산 / Seoraksan 江陵 / 강릉 / Gangneung 平昌 / 평창 / Pyeongchang 南怡岛 / 남이섬 / Nami Island 束草 / 속초 / Sokcho 正东津 / 정동진 / Jeongdongjin

📖 历史背景 Historical Background

Ancient homeland of the Yemaek (예맥/濊貊) tribes. Divided at the 38th parallel in 1945 — the northern half remains part of the DPRK as North Gangwon Province. The 2018 PyeongChang Winter Olympics carried deep reunification symbolism, with North and South Korean athletes marching together under a unified flag. The province's mountains have been sacred in Korean shamanism and Buddhism for millennia.

庆尚道 / 경상도 / Gyeongsang Province

Southeast Korea

Southeastern region encompassing Daegu, Gyeongju, Andong, Tongyeong, and Geoje. Home to the ancient Silla Dynasty capital and some of Korea's most significant historical sites.

庆州 / 경주 / Gyeongju 安东河回村 / 안동 하회마을 / Andong Hahoe 统营 / 통영 / Tongyeong 大邱 / 대구 / Daegu

📖 历史背景 Historical Background

Gyeongju (庆州) was the capital of the Silla Dynasty (57 BC–935 AD) for nearly 1,000 years — one of the longest-ruling dynasties in world history. Bulguksa Temple and Seokguram Grotto (751 AD) represent the golden age of Unified Silla. Andong (安东) was the heartland of Joseon-era Confucian scholars and yangban aristocracy. The region also encompassed the ancient Gaya Confederacy (가야, 42–562 AD), known for its ironwork and unique burial mounds.

全罗道 / 전라도 / Jeolla Province

Korea's Food Capital

Southwestern Korea, renowned as the country's culinary heartland. From Jeonju's birthplace of bibimbap to Suncheon Bay's ecological wetlands, this region rewards travelers seeking authentic Korean food and natural beauty.

全州 / 전주 / Jeonju 光州 / 광주 / Gwangju 顺天湾 / 순천만 / Suncheon Bay 丽水 / 여수 / Yeosu 潭阳 / 담양 / Damyang

📖 历史背景 Historical Background

Western territory of the ancient Baekje Kingdom (百济). Birthplace of the Donghak Peasant Revolution (1894, 东学农民运动), Korea's first large-scale democratic uprising against feudal corruption and foreign interference. Gwangju is hallowed ground for Korean democracy — the May 18th Gwangju Democratization Movement (1980, 光州民主化运动/광주민주화운동) saw citizens rise against martial law, with hundreds killed. The region's culinary supremacy traces to its fertile Honam Plain and centuries of rice-culture abundance.

忠清道 / 충청도 / Chungcheong Province

Central Korea

Central Korea spanning both North and South Chungcheong provinces. Home to the science city of Daejeon, the ancient Baekje Kingdom capitals of Gongju and Buyeo, and the scenic river valley of Danyang.

大田 / 대전 / Daejeon 公州 / 공주 / Gongju 扶余 / 부여 / Buyeo 丹阳 / 단양 / Danyang 保宁 / 보령 / Boryeong

📖 历史背景 Historical Background

Seat of the Baekje Kingdom's later capitals: Gongju (공주/公州, known as Ungjin 熊津, 475–538 AD) and Buyeo (부여/扶余, known as Sabi 泗沘, 538–660 AD). The kingdom fell in 660 AD to the Silla-Tang alliance at the Battle of Hwangsanbeol. The UNESCO World Heritage Baekje Historic Areas preserve royal tombs, fortress walls, and temple sites from this sophisticated ancient kingdom that significantly influenced Japanese art and architecture.

京畿道 & DMZ / 경기도 / Gyeonggi-do

Greater Seoul & Border Zone

The province surrounding Seoul, home to 13M+ people in satellite cities. The northern border area includes the DMZ — the world's most heavily fortified border and one of Korea's most unique tourist experiences.

板门店 / 판문점 / Panmunjom (DMZ) 水原华城 / 수원화성 / Suwon Hwaseong 坡州 / 파주 / Paju 杨平 / 양평 / Yangpyeong

📖 历史背景 Historical Background

The Korean War (1950–53) devastated this region, and the DMZ was established by the 1953 Armistice Agreement, splitting the peninsula along the 38th parallel. The Joint Security Area (JSA) at Panmunjom remains the only place where North and South Korean soldiers stand face to face. Suwon Hwaseong Fortress (수원화성/水原华城) was built 1794–1796 by King Jeongjo (正祖/정조) as a planned city to honor his father Crown Prince Sado — now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and masterpiece of late Joseon military architecture.

  • 🎢 Everland / Caribbean Bay theme parks
  • 🏘️ Korean Folk Village in Yongin (용인)
  • 🎨 Pocheon Art Valley quarry lake

📅 推荐行程 Suggested Itinerary

Day 1: DMZ / JSA tour (advance booking required, passport needed) → Imjingak Peace Park → Paju Book City or Premium Outlets
Day 2: Suwon Hwaseong fortress morning walk → Hwaseong Haenggung Palace → Suwon Galbi lunch → Korean Folk Village afternoon

🌍 Why Foreigners Love ItDMZ/JSA tours are a bucket-list item. Suwon Hwaseong fortress (UNESCO) offers a half-day trip from Seoul. Paju for DMZ-adjacent experiences.
