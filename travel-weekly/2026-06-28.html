• Domestic hotel investment is accelerating into tier-2/3 cities (64% of new keys), even as business hotel demand shows early softness.

• The spiritual tourism push (54 projects, ₹1,700Cr/~$204M) and monsoon hidden-gem discovery (Shimla +76%) point to a travel market diversifying beyond metro-leisure.

Visa-free entry to 25 nations from July 15 +1.2M projected — US, Germany, Australia, Brazil, South Korea included. 60-day stay for leisure travel only.

• Domestic travel bifurcation is sharp: Busan foreign arrivals surged +44.9% while Jeju declined –3.2% overall (domestic –7.2%, foreign +30.5%).

• AI-driven travel planning and short-distance "coolcation" patterns dominate consumer behavior this summer, with near-distance and playcation as leading travel modes.

Hallyu consumption at record high +73.7% YoY — May Hallyu spending 1.413T KRW (~$1.02B). Covers performances, shopping, cultural experiences, sports. Third consecutive month above 1T KRW.
Coolcation and playcation dominate summer demand 1 in 3 — Near-distance travel dominant this summer. Long-distance recovery gradual (+32% WoW bookings). "Coolcation," "playcation," and "nostalgia travel" are the season's keywords.
Busan thrives as Jeju struggles domestically Busan +44.9% / Jeju –3.2% — Busan foreign arrivals surged +44.9% (Jan–May), while Jeju overall May visitors dropped 3.2% YoY — domestic –7.2% offsetting foreign +30.5% growth.

• Japan's departure tax tripling (¥1,000→¥3,000/~$21) and 400% visa fee increase take effect July 1 — the most aggressive overtourism pricing signal in Asia.

• The Osaka-Kansai region is shattering pre-pandemic spending records thanks to the weak yen, even as nationwide arrivals growth normalizes.

• Policy attention is shifting from volume to value: the "Unforgettable Campaign" rebrands tourism strategy, while local governments gain power to ban private lodging.

Departure tax triples, visa fees rise 400% ¥3,000 (~$21) from Jul 1 — Revenue earmarked for tourism measures. Alongside visa fee increases, this is Japan's most significant price signal for inbound demand management.
Osaka-Kansai shatters spending records record spending — Weak yen drives historic spending boom across the Kansai region, with per-visitor spending far exceeding pre-pandemic levels.
STR local ban law advances local govt power — Japan now allows local governments to ban private lodging. Yamanashi's snow monkey park caps visitors at 2,000/day after overcrowding and behavioral issues.
