💡 AIRBNB APAC IMPLICATIONS
1. Korea: peak demand window is NOW — Golden Week + Labor Day convergence means a 2-week surge of Japanese and Chinese visitors. Korea is #1 destination for both markets. Prioritize Seoul/Busan/Jeju listings with Chinese/Japanese-language amenities. KTO's "Shelter Sale Festa" expanding to foreigners — potential co-marketing channel. Also watch: Airbnb x HYBE CORTIS fan-accommodation partnership targeting Gen Z inbound — a novel acquisition channel.
2. Japan: regulatory tightening accelerates — Kyoto considering short-term rental prohibition zones; Osaka "closely monitoring" 2,817 minpaku facilities; nationwide centralized management system from 2026. New accommodation taxes (Kyoto Apr 1, Nagano/Kumamoto/Miyazaki June) and departure fee tripling to ¥3,000 (July) signal a higher-cost, higher-compliance environment. Full compliance verification essential before peak season.
3. China-Korea corridor is the growth engine — China outbound fully rebounded to 101M passengers; Korea is #1 destination. Q1 2026: 1.45M Chinese visitors to Korea (+29%). Airbnb should ensure Korean host readiness for Chinese guests (WeChat Pay, Chinese listings, Alipay integration). Meanwhile, China-Japan travel collapsing (−54% to −60.7%) — deprioritize Japan listings for Chinese audience.
4. India: inbound headwind, domestic opportunity — Iran War −15–20% inbound is a real drag, but outbound is booming (11.4% CAGR, $23.4B in 2026). Indian travelers increasingly seeking Southeast Asia, Europe, and Réunion (new IndiGo route Apr 29). Airbnb should strengthen listings in Thailand, Vietnam, Korea, and emerging European destinations for Indian outbound demand. Domestic: Spiti, Kashmir, Sikkim trending — build supply in these regions.
5. Competitor pressure intensifying across APAC — Yanolja/Hereoette consolidating Korea domestic; Hereoette launching live commerce and Osaka hotel promos. In Japan, jalan.net tops NPS at 4.2. In China, SAMR antitrust investigation into Trip.com could reshape OTA dynamics. Airbnb's competitive moat: global inventory + unique experiences. Double down on what OTAs can't replicate.
