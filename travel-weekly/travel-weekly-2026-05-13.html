💡 Airbnb APAC Implications
1. India: Double down on first-time booker acquisition. Two consecutive quarters of 50% India growth + 75% first-timer surge = the flywheel is spinning. With Modi pushing domestic travel and 90% of Indian travelers planning summer bookings, Airbnb's unique/village property supply and flexible alternatives are perfectly positioned. Priority: expand verified supply in tier-2/3 cities (Kochi, Northeast), where emerging demand is unserved by traditional hotels.
2. Korea: Leverage "K-discount" as inbound grows to 20M+. Korea's sub-0.60 PLI vs Japan/Singapore is a structural advantage. Busan's positioning as a "stay city" (BTS concert Jun 12–13, BOF festival) creates high-demand accommodation moments — Airbnb-Hybe K-pop collaboration should be accelerated and expanded. Airbnb Japan searches for Korea up 5x YoY confirms cross-border discovery.
3. China inbound: Prepare supply and UX for the visa-free wave. With 8.32M visa-free entries in Q1 and Ctrip targeting 200M cumulative visitors by 2030, inbound accommodation demand from Western/South American markets (Brazil now visa-free) is accelerating. Airbnb must ensure: multi-language host support, foreign payment integration, and surface availability of listings with amenities relevant to international guests.
4. Singapore + SEA: Capture share as spending softens. Singapore's "more arrivals, less spend" dynamic favors Airbnb's value proposition vs. hotels. The S$740M STB spend on transformation (partly tech-focused) may create partnership opportunities. Scoot's Indonesia capacity expansion (+22%) opens new feeder demand corridors.
5. Japan: Experiences pipeline matters more than accommodation. With Hiroshima-Miyajima tours ranked #2 globally and the experience market at $271B (growing to $340B+ by 2029), Japan's experience economy is a major opportunity. Airbnb's experiences pivot from local hosts to larger operators is timely — gastronomy and nature tours are Japan's fastest growing segments.
