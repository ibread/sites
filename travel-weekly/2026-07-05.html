💡 Airbnb APAC Implications

Japan's China pullback is redrawing the entire Asian inbound map −48% China to Japan — With Chinese arrivals to Japan forecast to halve to 4.8M (from 9.3M) and Korea now Japan's #1 source market, demand is redistributing — Korea, Vietnam, and Southeast Asia are absorbing Chinese outbound (Korea #1 for Chinese travelers, Seoul heat doubling). Airbnb should treat this as a multi-year supply-planning signal, not a blip: leisure host readiness in Korea and SEA matters more than ever.
Experience-led demand is precisely Airbnb's structural right-to-win +$433/person — Across every market this week, the winning traveler is choosing the experience over the destination: India Gen Z (92% want a stay reflecting personal taste), K-culture travelers spend $433 more per person, China's immersive economy displacing generic sightseeing. This is exactly the trip type where unique stays + Experiences outperform commodity hotels — the case for the co-created stay is getting stronger.
Korea's domestic-guest deregulation is the single biggest supply unlock in APAC 180 nights/yr pilot — Legislation to let Korean residents (내국인) use Airbnb-style urban listings is formally advancing, and Busan's regulatory sandbox (180 nights/year) is already triggering an investor property rush in Gwangalli. If enacted, this converts Korea from a foreigner-only market to full domestic demand — Airbnb should be positioning host acquisition ahead of the legislation, not after.
Homestay/non-standard lodging is outgrowing hotels across the region's biggest markets +45% daily — China's Mujia Minsu summer bookings growing +45% daily WoW (boutique county-level stays pre-selling out); India's vacation rental market projected to grow 19.2% CAGR to $19.9B by 2035; Korea hanok full-unit rentals surging over hotels. The structural shift toward non-standardized lodging is Airbnb's tailwind — but domestic OTAs (Mujia, Samsamm2, Dandan Homes) are racing for the same supply.

🇮🇳 India (भारत)

• A new Airbnb report crystallizes India's Gen Z shift: 87% prefer trips under a week, 7 in 10 favor three short trips over one annual holiday, and 92% want a stay reflecting their personal taste — a decisive move from quantity to experiential quality.

• India's outbound market hit sudden turbulence — PM Modi's appeal to defer foreign travel (~−15% bookings) collides with airline international capacity cuts at peak season, even as outbound had just overtaken inbound in Q1.

• Inbound softened (FTAs −14.4% YoY in April on West Asia tensions), but structural fundamentals are strong: multiple-entry eVisa now live, RevPAR +22-24% YoY, and non-metro cities driving 63% of outbound.

🇯🇵 Japan (日本)

• Japan's July 1 visa-fee overhaul (+400% single-entry to ¥15,000/~$93) and tripled departure tax (¥3,000/~$18.60) — first revision since 1978 — deliberately target China, India, and Vietnam while sparing visa-exempt Western/Korean markets.

• The China pullback is now structural: May arrivals fell 3.6% YoY, JTB cut the full-year forecast to 41.4M (−2.8%), Chinese arrivals forecast to halve to 4.8M, and Korea has replaced China as the #1 source market.

• Policy is decisively shifting from volume to value: a national 100-area overtourism plan, a Nov 1 airport-refund tax-free reform, and Kyoto's ¥10,000/night lodging tax all signal managed, premium-tier tourism.

Visa fees rise up to 400%, departure tax triples from Jul 1 ¥15,000 / ¥3,000 — Single-entry visa fees jump 5x to ¥15,000 (~$93), multiple-entry to ¥30,000 (~$186), departure tax to ¥3,000 (~$18.60). Economists say strong repeat-visitor appeal means demand is unlikely to fall. CNBC
Korea overtakes China as Japan's #1 source market −48% China forecast — May arrivals dipped 3.6% YoY to 3.56M on China/HK declines; JTB cut 2026 to 41.4M (−2.8%). Chinese arrivals are forecast to halve to 4.8M, cementing Japan's official pivot to Western and Korean markets. Skift / JTB
